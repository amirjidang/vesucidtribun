Did Carlo Ancelotti win the World Cup?

Publish date: 2024-08-29

Carlo Ancelotti is an Italian professional football manager and former player who is the manager of La Liga club Real Madrid.

Carlo Ancelotti Cavaliere was born on June 10, 1959, in Reggiolo, Italy as the son of Giuseppe Ancelotti. He has played as a midfielder, initially started his professional career with Italian club Parma.

Carlo Ancelotti won a Serie A title and four Coppa Italia titles after moving to Roma. He has also played for the late 1980s Milan team, with which he won two league titles and two European Cups, among others.

Carlo Ancelotti played for the Italian national team and has made appearances in two FIFA World Cups, as well as UEFA Euro 1988. He switched to a managerial career and has worked for Reggiana, Parma, Juventus, Milan, Chelsea, Paris Saint-Germain, Real Madrid, Bayern Munich, Napoli and Everton.

Carlo Ancelotti has proven his point as one of the most successful managers in the world and is one of only three managers to have won the UEFA Champions League three times (twice with AC Milan and once with Real Madrid). He is also one of only two to have managed teams in four finals.

Carlo Ancelotti has won the FIFA Club World Cup twice while managing Milan and Real Madrid. He is also one of seven people to have won the European Cup or Champions League as both a player and a manager.

Carlo Ancelotti is the father of two children: a daughter named Katia; as well as a son, Davide, who was his assistant coach at Everton, and now also at Real Madrid.

Did Carlo Ancelotti win the World Cup?

Yes, Carlo Ancelotti won the World Cup. He has won the FIFA Club World Cup twice while managing Milan and Real Madrid. Carlo has also won the UEFA Champions League three times (twice with AC Milan and once with Real Madrid).
