Haley Jones Parents Patrick And Monique Jones Were Both Basketball Players

Publish date: 2024-08-27

Haley Jones grew up with parents, Patrick And Monique Jones, who were both basketball players. Her mom and dad used to work at Santa Cruz High School.

At a young age, she picked up basketball skills from her parents, both well-known athletes in their youth days.

While in third grade, Haley began playing basketball; however, it was not the only sport she participated in. The youngster is also a standout in both soccer and gymnastics.

1. Haley Jones Parents Raised Her In The Gym Of Santa Cruz High School

Stanford basketball star Haley Jones grew up watching her parents, Patrick and Monique Jones, play basketball. Her parents were both renowned basketball players in their high school days.

Patrick and Monique were students at Santa Cruz High School, where they first met and eventually fell in love. Haley was born to them on 23 May 2001.

Later, they also served as coaches at Santa Cruz, where their daughter grew up. Haley used to be at practice every day, learning from her parents and the athletes. So she was completely immersed in the sport as a child. 

At home, it continued to be the same. As her parents prepared supper and the family settled in for the evening, Jones recalled that there was always a basketball game on television, whether it was an NBA, men’s, or women’s game.

In a conversation with Santa Cruz Sentinel, Haley shared she grew up in a gym, where she would show up for practice, hang out with the girls, and always have a ball in hand.

She committed fully to basketball in high school and attended Archbishop Mitty High School in San Jose.

With her outstanding performance at the highest levels of AAU basketball, she gained the attention of the finest college basketball programs in the nation.

Her father claims Haley is hardwired in a particular way with a desire to excel in the classroom and the basketball court but also has compassion. He noticed it about his daughter during open gym before Jonae’s high school career even started.

2. Haley Jones Father Patrick Was Basketball Player At Colorado College

Haley Jones has basketball in her blood. Her dad, Patrick, played basketball at Colorado College in his youth.

Growing up, Haley spent much of her childhood courtside. After finishing her homework, she’d listed in on the guidance doled out in practice.

Her dad also served as head coach at Santa Cruz High School for seventeen years. Jones was the varsity boys’ basketball coach for two seasons (2012 to 2014).

Throughout his coaching career, Patrick guided the Cardinals” boys varsity squad to a 19-9 overall record. Also, he helped the team to reach its second consecutive participation in the NorCal playoffs.

In the 2014 season, Santa Cruz was chosen to compete in the CCS Open Division after winning the SCCAL championship, and Jone received the SCCAL Coach of the Year award.

3. Haley Jones Mother Monique Was Head Coach At Santa Cruz High School

Haley Jones’s mother, Monique, was a collegiate basketball player at Metro State. She raised her daughter while also pursuing her coaching career.

Between 1997 and 2014, Monique and her husband served as head coaches at Santa Cruz High. There, she coached for the varsity girl basketball team.

In June 2014, athletic director Erik Redding released a statement claiming that the Jones officially resigned from their respective positions.

According to santacruzsportshistory.com, Monique and Patrick coached at Santa Cruz for almost two decades. They were also awarded Coach of the Year by the Santa Cruz Coast Athletic League in 2008-09.

Monique led the girls’ varsity team for the Cardinals to a 15-13 overall record in her last season. Santa Cruz has twice advanced to the CCS Division III quarterfinals under her leadership.

4. Her Brother Cameron Jones Plays At Linfield College

Haley Jones’ elder brother Cameron Jones is a basketball player at Linfield College in Oregon. He also played for Santa Cruz High School.

The two siblings grew up learning basketball skills with their parents. They used to practice with their mom and dad regularly on the court.

Cameron was once committed to Palma High School, where he was a three-year varsity basketball letter champion.

During his junior year, he was regarded as the most inspirational athlete on the state runner-up team. He also competed in two all-star matches.

Cameron was a member of the first-team all-Monterey Bay League-Gablian Division in basketball. He was also associated with the seven league championship teams.

Haley’s brother also received All-state recognition during his high school career. In 2021, he majored in management at Palma High School.

5. Haley Jones Family Has Long History In Sports

Haley Jones came from a family with an athletic background. Both of her parents were outstanding high school basketball coaches.

Similarly, her uncle Tyron James was a well-liked footballer at Clark University. So, it was natural for Haley to have an interest in sports.

Nevertheless, Haley’s cousin DeLisha Milton-Jones is a two-time FIBA World Cup gold medalist. Also, Milton-Jone won the Olympic gold medal twice (2000, 2008).

DeLisha is well-recognized for the unusual length of her arms, which give her a wingspan of 84 inches—typical for someone who is 7 feet tall.
