Success at NBC

Lester Holt’s career at NBC has been marked by numerous achievements and contributions to the field of journalism. Since joining the network in 2000, Holt has risen to prominence as one of the most respected news anchors in the industry. He initially started as a full-time anchor for “NBC Nightly News” and “Today,” gradually expanding his role to include hosting his own daily newscast and becoming the co-anchor of “Weekend Today.”

However, it was in 2015 that Holt made history by becoming the permanent anchor for the weekday edition of “NBC Nightly News,” succeeding Brian Williams. This groundbreaking appointment made him the first African-American to anchor a major American weekday nightly news program alone. Holt’s tenure as anchor of “NBC Nightly News” has been predominantly successful, with his journalistic expertise shining through in his coverage of significant events and his role as a moderator in presidential debates.
