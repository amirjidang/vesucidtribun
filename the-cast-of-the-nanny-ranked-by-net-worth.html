The Cast Of The Nanny Ranked By Net Worth

Publish date: 2024-08-14

The Nanny was one of the most iconic sitcoms of the '90s. The series starred Fran Drescher as Fran Fine, a nanny who took care of three children in Manhattan. Drescher co-created the show with her former husband Peter Marc Jacobson. Much of the show's inspiration came from Drescher's own life growing up in Queens, New York.

During its run from 1993 to 1999, the show was hugely successful. It earned a Rose d'Or and an Emmy Award out of 12 nominations in total. Drescher herself was nominated for an Emmy and two Golden Globes. Her character's fashion has also been part of the show's legend. This has caused Drescher to become a fashion icon.

In addition to Drescher, the show featured an incredible cast. But which one is the richest? Let's find out.

8 Benjamin Salisbury - $500 Thousand

Benjamin Salisbury is best known for his role as Brighton Sheffield on The Nanny. His on-camera career began in elementary school when he appeared in the film Captain Ron. Salisbury went on to appear in projects such as Shimmer, Iron Will, Kirk, Promised Land, D3: The Mighty Ducks, Guide to the World of Family Computers, Software, and Games, Sim0ne, Red Zone, Numb3rs, and On the Brink. He also worked as a voice actor, playing characters in animated projects like Virtual Oz, Toto Lost in New York, The Nome Prince and the Magic Belt, Who Stole Santa, The Oz Kids, and Christmas in Oz.

7 Madeline Zima - $1.5 Million

Although Madeline Zima is mostly recognized as Grace Sheffield from The Nanny, she acted in several other projects. Zima has appeared in films such as The Hand That Rocks the Cradle, Mr. Nanny, 'Til There Was You, A Cinderella Story, Streak, The Collector, and Crazy Kind of Love. She began her career by appearing in commercials when she was still in diapers. Zima has appeared in television shows such as Law and Order, Touched by an Angel, Lethal Vows, The Nightmare Room, Gilmore Girls, 7th Heaven, Strong Medicine, Ghost Whisperer, Grey's Anatomy, Californication, Heroes, Royal Pains, and The Vampire Diaries.

6 Lauren Lane - $2 Million

The role of C.C. Babcock is what Lauren Lane is best known for. Her time on The Nanny isn't the only acting she's done. Lane has performed in stage productions such as Dinner with Friends, The Vagina Monologues, and August: Osage County. Prior to The Nanny, Lane played Sgt. Chris Novak in the series Hunter in 1991. From 1991 to 1992, she portrayed Julie Rayburn in the show L.A. Law. Her other acting projects include Interface, Positive I.D., Nervous Ticks, Perry Mason: The Case of the Skin-Deep Scandal, Gen, The Cutting Room, South Beach, Burke's Law, Duckman, Partners, and more.

5 Daniel Davis - $2 Million

Daniel Davis experienced his career breakthrough when he played Niles on The Nanny. His television debut came when he was only 11 in a local broadcast program called Betty’s Little Rascals. After performing and teaching at the American Conservatory Theatre, Davis launched his career by playing Elliot Carrington on the show Texas, which aired from 1980 to 1981. In addition to The Nanny, he also appeared in television shows such as Civil Wars, Gotham, The Blacklist, and Murder, She Wrote. Davis has also acted in films including Blind Justice, K-9, The Hunt for Red October, Thru the Moebius Strip, and The Prestige.

4 Renée Taylor - $3 Million

Prior to her role on The Nanny, Taylor starred as Doris Tupper on the TV series Dream On. She also played Helen Mitchell in 1993's Daddy Dearest. That same year, she began her most famous role as Sylvia Fine in The Nanny. Her role as Fran's mother brought her much acclaim and success. Taylor also appeared in television shows such as St. Elsewhere, How I Met Your Mother, and Shake It Up!. In addition to on-camera work, Taylor also found success as a voiceover actor. She has appeared in projects such as Dr. Dolittle 2, Ice Age: The Meltdown, Allen Gregory, and Bob's Burgers. During her career, Taylor was nominated for and Academy Award in 1971. She was also nominated for three Primetime Emmy Awards, winning one in 1973.

3 Charles Shaughnessy - $3 Million

Charles Shaughnessy played the role of Maxwell Sheffield on The Nanny. His character had a brilliant chemistry with Drescher's. So much so that the two ended up working together several times after the series ended. They both acted together in Living With Fran and Happily Divorced. Prior to The Nanny, Shaughnessy played Shane Donovan on the soap opera Days of Our Lives from 1984 to 1992. He won three Soap Opera Digest Awards in 1985, 1986, and 1988. He is also the recipient of a Daytime Emmy Award and a Broadway World Boston Theater Award.

2 Nicholle Tom - $13 Million

Nicholle Tom is best known for her role as Maggie Sheffield in The Nanny. She has also appeared in the film Beethoven as well as its sequel. Tom played the role of Ryce Newton. She appeared in an episode of The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air, which was her first experience with acting. Tom also played the role of Sue in the popular series Beverly Hills, 90210. Tom isn't the only one in her family who has the acting bug. Her brother appeared in The Young and the Restless and her sister appeared in One Life to Live.

1 Fran Drescher - $25 Million

With her role as Fran Fine on The Nanny, Drescher became a household name. Drescher isn't just known as a talented actress and comedian, she's also known as the co-creator of both The Nanny and Happily Divorced with former husband Peter Marc Jacobson. In addition to airing 146 episodes during its six season run, The Nanny went on to become a popular show in syndication. Drescher paid $1.225 million for a beachfront home in Malibu, California in 1997. As of today, the property is estimated to be worth between $7-10 million.
