The 10 Most Expensive Marvel Movies Ever Made

The history of Marvel movies dates back to the early 2000s when Sam Raimi and Ang Lee first took upon the daunting task of introducing Spider-Man to the world with Tobey Maguire’s Peter Parker. While the trilogy has its fitting end, Kevin Feige had already begun working on the Marvel Cinematic Universe, which first began with Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark in Iron Man, which was released in 2008. It forever changed the history of Marvel movies and became a successful superhero series that fans waited to see in theaters. Along with detailed planning for each film, the Marvel production house commissions large budgets for movies with an A-list star cast.

From the X-Men universe movies that are released alongside the MCU to the Avengers series that made crossover superhero movies a reality, each Marvel film has been handsomely rewarded for its enormous budget. Let’s take a look at the most expensive Marvel movies ever made.

X-Men: Days Of Future Past (2014)- $205 million

 

One of the highest-grossing movies of X-Men that does not feature the character Deadpool, X-Men: Days Of Future Past reunited the X-Men to travel back in time and save the world. According to Screen Rant, made on a $205 million budget, it grossed $747.8 million worldwide and $233 million at the domestic box office.

X-Men: The Last Stand (2006)- $210 million

 

X-Men: The Last Stand was the third film in its series and its highest-grossing one at the time, making $460.4 million. The film was a moderate success and remained the highest-budget X-Men movie to date, as it spent $210 million on production. It followed multiple storylines and also gave a preview of Jane as the mighty Dark Phoenix.

The Avengers (2012)- $220 million

 

The entire MCU and storylines led to the big show when The Avengers hit theaters in 2012. It was Marvel’s highest-budget movie of the time, and it handsomely paid off as the film garnered $1.5 billion everywhere. It saw the original Avengers: Captain America, Iron Man, Black Widow, The Hulk, Thor, and Hawkeye team together for the first time, which eventually led to the great endgame.

Captain America: Civil War (2016)- $250 million

 

Joe and Anthony Russo returned as the directors for Captain America: Civil War after the success of The Winter Soldier. It became the highest-grossing Captain America movie and the first to breach the $1 billion-dollar mark, as noted by Deadline. Along with Chris Evans as Captain America, the film gave a glimpse into the future of the Avengers and introduced the world to Tom Holland’s Spider-Man.

Thor: Love And Thunder (2022)- $250 million

 

Taika Waititi took the reins of his second Marvel movie as a director with Thor: Love And Thunder. A superhero and love story, the film brought Natalie Portman’s Jane back to the screen as Thor herself. The fourth film was filmed in Sydney, Australia, on a massive budget of $250 million. It became the sixth highest-grossing film of 2022 by making $760 million in theaters.

The Amazing Spider-Man (2014)- $255 million

 

The Amazing Spider-Man 2 was the second and last time Andrew Garfield played a solo Spider-Man in a film. The second installment of The Amazing Spider-Man collected $709 million worldwide. Directed by Marc Webb, the film saw three villains conspiring against Peter Parker, who needs to save his loved ones. It also starred Emma Stone, Dane DeHaan, and Jamie Foxx.

Spider-Man 3 (2007)- $258 million

 

While Marvel was on the brink of bringing its first phase to the audiences, Sam Raimi had already finished a successful Spider-Man trilogy starring Tobey Maguire. As mentioned by Forbes, made on a $258 million budget, the film became the highest-grossing superhero movie of its time by collecting $336 million in North America and $890 worldwide.

Avengers: Infinity War (2018)- $321 million

 

The conclusion to the Marvel Phase 4 began with Avengers: Infinity War when the characters split into different realms and tried to protect the world from Thanos. The movie's budget was over $300 million as it had every superhero from the MCU come together for a fitting ending. It had the biggest opening weekend for a movie and even crossed the $2 billion mark in sales.

Avengers: Endgame (2019)- $356 million

 

Joe and Anthony Russo gave audiences the perfect ending with the second part and follow-up of Infinity War. Endgame has several notable cameos and a roaring finale that helped it peak at $2.7 billion. It became the highest-grossing movie ever, but Avatar dethroned it after it was re-released in theaters.

Avengers: Age Of Ultron (2015)- $365 million

 

Avengers: Age Of Ultron may not be the highest-grossing Marvel movie to date, but it remains a memorable one not only for its introduction to new Avengers but also for its staggering budget. Produced for $365 million, the world first saw Elizabeth Olsen in her role as the Scarlet Witch and Aaron Taylor-Johnson as Pietro. The film made slightly less than The Avengers and collected $1.405 billion worldwide, as stated by Collider.

Other notable expensive Marvel movies include Spider-Man: Far From Home, Spider-Man 2, and Black Panther. While the budgets of Marvel movies have continued to increase over the last decade, the series has entertained audiences for many years and kept fans on their toes for new releases.
