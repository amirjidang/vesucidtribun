David Tennant Biography

David Tennant is a Scottish and British actor who was born David John McDonald on April 18, 1971 in Bathgate, West Lothian, Scotland. David was born into a prosperous family. Sandy McDonald served as the ‘Moderator of the General Assembly of the Church of Scotland.’ Helen McLeod was his mother’s name. David grew up in Ralston, Renfrewshire, with his two siblings. His father was a local minister at the time.

David Tennant Education

David received his primary and secondary education in the same city. After finishing primary school at Ralston Primary and Paisley Grammar School, he went on to study acting at the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama.

Career

David had wanted to be an actor since he was three years old. He was a huge fan of the TV show ‘Doctor Who,’ which inspired him to pursue a career in acting. He began acting as a child and has appeared in numerous stage plays. At the age of 11, he wowed actress Edith MacArthur with his extraordinary acting abilities. His career, however, began when he was 16 years old. He appeared in a short film produced by Glasgow Health Board. He appeared in small plays, TV shows, and movies over the next few years, but he never found success. He rose to prominence and acclaim in 1994 for his role in the Scottish drama series ‘Talkin’ Over The Asylum.’ This TV show did well at the box office and brought him much-needed attention.

In 1996, he joined the Royal Shakespeare Company and was cast in the play ‘As You Like It.’ It increased his popularity, and he landed some major roles as a result. Over the next decade, he appeared in a number of blockbuster films and television shows, including Blackpool, Casanova, Bright Young Things, Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, and a few others. He had established himself as a superstar by the early 2000s, but there was still something he wanted to accomplish. His dream came true in 2005 when he was cast in his favorite TV show, ‘Doctor Who.’
