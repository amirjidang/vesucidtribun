In 1988, David kicked off his professional career after he was drafted as the 7th round by the Los Angeles Dodgers. He made his MLB debut on June 29, 2002, and stroke out as a pinch hitter.

The Dodgers sold Ross to the Pittsburgh Pirates on March 30, 2005. After playing 40 games with Pirates, he was traded to San Diego Padres. With a .203 batting average and 17 home runs, he finished the 2007 season.

In 2013, The Boston Red Sox won the ‘2013 World Series Championship’ against St. Louis Cardinals. Ross played as Jon Lester’s Catcher in 2014. On May 9, 2015, he recorded a perfect inning and played as a pitcher for the first time in his professional career. After winning the 2016 World Series Championship, he announced his retirement in 2017.

In 2005, he married his long-time girlfriend Hyla Ross who is a pediatric nurse. The couple is blessed with the son Cole Ross and two daughters Landri Ross and Harper Ross.
