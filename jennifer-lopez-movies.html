For more than 20 years, Jennifer Lopez has drawn large crowds to theaters with her charismatic acting, singing, and dancing abilities.

She has starred in thrillers like Anaconda and romantic comedies like Maid In Manhattan, and she has even attempted more somber dramatic roles in her eclectic career.

Today, we'll take a look at how Lopez progressed from a rising star to a genuine box office success, with career film revenues of over $3 billion worldwide, starting with her breakthrough performance in Selena and continuing with her 2005 monster comedy hit Monster-In-Law.

As of early 2024, Lopez's net worth is estimated at a staggering $400 million, with her work in Hollywood a significant factor in the size of her fortune.

Although the Bronx-born entertainer is often thought of primarily as a singer, there are a number of successful Jennifer Lopez movies, and these are the highest-grossing.

10 Out of Sight (1998): $77.7 Million

Jennifer Lopez played a U.S. Marshal pursuing a charming bank robber in the 1998 crime thriller Out Of Sight, co-starring George Clooney.

Out Of Sight, which cost $48 million, made $77.7 million worldwide. While not a great box office success, it was Lopez's first significant breakout performance after minor roles in action films such as Anaconda.

Out Of Sight showcased Lopez as a diverse actress, with critics praising her ability to match Clooney's magnetism.

Though it was not her highest-grossing hit, it demonstrated Lopez's ability to thrive in higher-budget, star-driven films, and elevated her to A-list status.

9 The Wedding Planner (2001): $94.7 Million

Jennifer Lopez established her rom-com appeal in 2001's The Wedding Planner, which also starred Matthew McConaughey.

This hilarious and engaging film about a wedding planner falling for one of her clients cost $35 million to produce, and grossed $94.7 million worldwide. The film was one of Lopez's biggest commercial successes during her early film career.

The Wedding Planner established Lopez as a highly bankable figure capable of attracting crowds to romantic comedies on her own star power, grossing $60 million in the United States alone.

Along with Maid In Manhattan, it solidified her place as a genre queen in the 2000s.

8 Anaconda (1997): $136.8 Million

Anaconda, a 1997 thriller, was one of Jennifer Lopez's first box office hits, in which she portrays the director of a film crew who chances upon a gigantic snake.

Anaconda earned $136.8 million worldwide against a $45 million budget. With its blend of suspense and violence, Anaconda demonstrated Lopez's ability to play characters other than dramatic ones.

Despite not developing into a breakout performance, Lopez's supporting turn in this creature feature, following critical acclaim for Selena, helped establish her as a budding star.

Although it wasn't her biggest-grossing hit, Anaconda's earnings showed that Lopez could attract audiences to effects-driven event films early in her blockbuster career.

7 Monster-In-Law (2005): $154.7 Million

To date, Jennifer Lopez's list of highest-grossing films includes the comedy Monster-In-Law (2005).

Also starring Jane Fonda, Monster-In-Law grossed $154.7 million worldwide on the back of a $43 million budget.

This film reinforced Lopez's reputation as a rom-com star, showcasing her charisma and comedic timing.

Monster-In-Law became her biggest comic splash at the time, demonstrating Lopez's ability to deliver as a headliner at the height of her celebrity.

6 Hustlers (2019): $157.6 Million

Despite having a $20 million budget, the 2019 crime drama Hustlers, which starred Jennifer Lopez as an exotic dancer, raked in $157.6 million worldwide.

Hustlers became an unexpected box office hit, with Lopez directing an acclaimed cast that included Constance Wu, Julia Stiles, and Lizzo.

With a $33.2 million domestic debut, it became the highest-grossing STX picture ever.

Despite having a small budget, Hustlers became one of the most successful independent films of 2019 and one of the most profitable movies of Lopez's career, thanks to its widespread distribution and star power.

5 Shall We Dance? (2004): $170.4 Million

In Shall We Dance?, a 2004 romantic comedy, Richard Gere plays a bored married man who becomes interested in Jennifer Lopez's character Paulina, a dance instructor.

Against a $50 million budget, the movie rustled up $170.4 million at the box office globally.

Shall We Dance? showcased Lopez's developing comedic skills and on-screen connection with Gere, even though it wasn't her biggest romantic comedy smash.

At the height of her rom-com fame in the early 2000s, Shall We Dance? gave Lopez one of her most lucrative performances, solidifying her status as a bankable leading woman.

4 Antz (1998): $171.8 Million

With a $105 million budget, Antz was DreamWorks Animation's first computer-animated movie, and it debuted in 1998.

With Woody Allen and Sharon Stone lending their voices, the ant-focused story brought in $171.8 million globally.

Even though Antz was the studio's first computer-generated feature, it was still lucrative at the box office, even though its earnings were modest compared to later DreamWorks hits.

That same year, Antz faced competition from Pixar's A Bug's Life, yet it was a strong opening act for the up-and-coming animation company.

Despite not being a huge hit, its worldwide total showed promise for DreamWorks' future and the potential of CGI pictures.

Antz cleared the way for other significant animated feature films.

3 Maid In Manhattan (2002): $174.0 Million

Lopez starred in the 2002 romantic comedy Maid In Manhattan, as a hotel maid who falls in love with Ralph Fiennes' character, a wealthy politician.

Maid In Manhattan cost $55 million to make and was a commercial success, grossing $174 million worldwide. Maid In Manhattan was one of Lopez's most successful films during her rise to stardom in Hollywood.

With its Cinderella-esque plot, Lopez's inherent charisma, and her on-screen rapport with Fiennes, it drew in crowds.

Maid In Manhattan, Lopez's highest-grossing rom-com, demonstrated her peak box office prowess with respect to crowd-pleasing romantic comedies in the early 2000s.

2 Home (2015): $386 Million

The 2015 animated sci-fi comedy Home followed the Boov, an alien race that invaded Earth.

DreamWorks Animation produced Home on a $135 million budget, grossing $386 million worldwide. While a solid result, it was regarded as lackluster compared to DreamWorks classics such as Shrek.

However, Home achieved international success, generating $208 million from overseas markets.

Despite not being a huge hit, Home resonated more worldwide for DreamWorks – because of the voice acting of stars such as Rihanna and Jim Parsons, and its spectacular visuals.

Home's global total ultimately exceeded projections, resulting in a significant profit for the studio.

1 Ice Age: Continental Drift (2012): $887 Million

Ice Age: Continental Drift grossed a whopping $877.2 million globally in 2012, against a $95 million budget; it had the best global opening of the series, grossing $126.9 million.

Thanks to an incredible $715.9 million in overseas revenue, Ice Age: Continental Drift became the year's biggest animated feature film.

Continental Drift also became the most profitable chapter in the Ice Age franchise, accounting for 81.6 percent of the total gross, and attracting significant interest from overseas audiences.

The Ice Age mammals rose to new franchise heights at the 2012 box office, demonstrating their continued global popularity.
