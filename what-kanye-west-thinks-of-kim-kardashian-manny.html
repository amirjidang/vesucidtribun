Kim Kardashian Wasn't Sure How Kanye West Would Respond To This Parenting Choice

Publish date: 2024-08-16

Highlights

  • Co-parenting has been challenging for Kim and Kanye, with both parents entering new relationships and Kanye's mental health struggles.
  • Kim Kardashian hires a male nanny to provide positive male role models for her children, particularly her son Saint. Kanye West is surprisingly supportive.
  • Despite some fans criticizing Kim's decision, Kanye embraced the new nanny and even directed him on how to raise his son.

Every week there is a different story online that references the latest development in the lives of Kim Kardashian and Kanye West. While many of them are rumors, there is an equal amount that comes from the sources themselves and can be verified as factual.

In an episode of the family's Hulu series The Kardashians, Kim Kardashian shared an interesting tidbit of information regarding the care of her children, specifically her oldest son Saint. As the icon told her family, she made a somewhat drastic decision, and she wasn't sure how Kanye was going to react to the news.

Kim Kardashian And Kanye West Have Shared Custody Of Their Four Kids

The infamous couple of Kim Kardashian and Kanye West share four children together, their two daughters North and Chicago, and two sons Saint and Psalm.

Fans frequently see the kids on the family television show and via paparazzi photos that make their way online. Throughout the years audiences have seen the children grow up and form their own unique personalities.

Kim and Kanye may have four children, but their eldest daughter North apparently likes to think she's the only one.

The couple married when their firstborn child North was just under a year old. After welcoming three more children into their lives, the couple ultimately separated and filed for divorce in 2021. The announcement to separate was highly publicized and brought an even higher level of attention to the couple.

The proceedings went on for a lengthy time, with Kim ultimately asking the courts to declare her legally single while the terms of the divorce continued to be hammered out.

Co-Parenting Has Been A Challenge For Kim And Kanye (And So Has The Distance)

The press has been all over the turmoil associated with the Kardashian-West custody battle and parenting woes since 2021. Not only have reports seen Kanye been jet-setting across the world, but both parents have entered into multiple new relationships since separating.

Additionally, having long suffered with his mental health, Kanye West has retreated back to some of his old ways, causing Kim Kardashian to beg West to stay on his medication for their children's sake after a series of unfortunate events took place overseas that made people question the rapper's wellbeing.

Even with Kanye's shortcomings, this isn't to say that Kim has been perfect in the journey either. While both parents have busy professional lives, Kim was also the first of the exes to dive into a new relationship.

When she was dating Pete Davidson, Kim spent a lot of time with the comedian, and so did her kids. Pete likely had to sign an NDA while dating Kim, and he even got tattoos for his then-girlfriend, and the two got pretty serious.

After their split, however, Kim's kids were left without a consistent male figure in their lives, leading Kim to an interesting choice.

To Combat Kanye's Lengthy Absences, Kim Hired A Male Nanny

With her children, most notably her son Saint, showing some behavioral challenges, Kim knew she needed to take steps to have positive male role models in her children's lives.

Knowing that Kanye isn't always reliable with his intense schedule, frequent traveling, and life circumstances, she decided to bring a male nanny on board to make up for any shortcomings that have occurred with Kanye's frequent absences and mental health struggles that have taken place throughout the years and post-divorce.

During a recent episode of 'The Kardashians,' Kim confessed she has more fun spending time with her sons than her daughters.

Understanding that her family is primarily female-dominated and that her schedule is jam-packed, Kim Kardashian made the decision to hire a male nanny instead of another female. After all was said and done, she stated that she was scared to tell Kanye because she didn't know what his reaction would be. However, the meet and greet and subsequent encounters went very well.

When Kanye dropped the children off at Kim's house one day, she initiated an introduction between the new hire and her ex-husband. To her surprise, Kanye didn't respond negatively.

Instead, the musician and the new nanny started to engage Saint in a game of football outside on Kim's lawn, with Kanye directing the nanny not to take it too easy on his son and to make sure he doesn't make things too simple for him, referencing the nanny picking something up for Saint that he could have done himself.

During the meeting, Kanye even commented to the nanny where he referenced what he wanted the nanny to do if he was going to be a person helping him raise his son.

When the decision was made to hire a new caregiver who would primarily care for her son Saint, Kim had a lot of worry about how the child's father would handle the news. Thankfully, it would seem that Kim's fears didn't come true with the way that Kanye embraced the individual and worked alongside him to orient him to the position in a friendly manner.

Yet not everyone was happy about Kim's decision; fans dragged Kim for hiring a manny, even though Kanye was totally supportive.
